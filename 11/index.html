Final Project Process


Demo Video

When choosing a final project, it was suggested that I should do something related to my hobbies. One of my favorite hobbies is photograpy, so I decided to do something based around my camera. Then I had the idea of doing some sort of controllable camera dolly that could move my camera back and forth. Finally, I wanted to make it attachable to my tripod in order to have it more eaisly lifted off the ground, allowing for more variety in what I photographed and filmed.

For my final project, I made a camera dolly that is attachable to my tripod. To the right is a test run video, time-lapsed. The code is written with Arduino on a metro board. I bought a metal zinc bar that would be strong enough to support my camera. Then I modeled a 3D piece that could fit in my tripod slot and could be attached to the metal bar. The code ran 2 stepper motors in the same direction so I placed 1 motor on each end of the bar with a string wrapped around both. I struggled to make the camera stable; I tried using various amounts of cardboard, but ended up just adding a bit of tape to the bottom of the camera to keep it from scraping.

The machine succeeded in filming my fish while sliding back and forth on the tripod bar (as shown in demo). The most challenging aspect was keeping the camera from rotating while moving. Solving this problem would require a different track design.

Kinetic Sculpture


For my kinetic sculpture, I wasn't sure what I wanted to construct, but I knew that motors from the kit would be a key aspect. I had one motor connected to the microprocessor from the previous assignment, but I added a second.

Eventually I decided to use two motors to create a paper airplane launcher. I considered using two of the other motors from the kit but I decided to test them side by side first. although the smaller nes had mor torque, my design favored speed instead.

Now that I knew what motors to use, I cut two 120mm cardboard circles and placed a rubberband around each for more friction. Next I cut two holes for the motors in an empty tissie box, and stuck two pencils through the side to support the heavy motors. Lastly, I adjusted the arduino code to spin the motors in opposite directions. The final result wasn't as powerfull as I had hopped, but it functioned correctly.

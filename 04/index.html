Kinetic Sculpture


For my kinetic sculpture, I wanted the motors from the kit to be a key aspect. I had one motor connected to the microcontroller from the previous assignment, but I chose to add a second to make a pair.

I decided to use two motors to create a paper airplane launcher. I considered using two of the larger motors from the kit so I tested them side by side. Although the smaller ones had more torque, my design favored speed instead due to the light-weight planes. I opted for the larger ones.

Now that I knew which motors to use, I cut two 120mm cardboard circles and placed a rubberband around each for more friction. Next I cut two holes for the motors in an empty tissue box and stuck two pencils through the side to support the heavy motors. Lastly, I adjusted the arduino code to spin the motors in opposite directions. The final result wasn't as powerful as I had hoped, but it functioned successfully.

Fusion 360


This section's assignment was to model at least 3 parts from our kits in Fusion 360 and assemble them together in some way. I wasn't sure at first what I wanted to do so I started with the rubber wheel from our kit and used the caliper to measure it. All of my models began as 2D sketches and were them extruded into 3D. Next, I modeled a bearing, followed by a screw, a nut, and lastly a base.

Using these parts, I decided to assemble them together in a sort of car. The bearing diameter was already the same size as the inner diameter of the wheel, and the screw & nut could just fit with the bearing, so I didn't need to scale up or down anything. Then I copied the wheels four times around the flat base.

The final result was 3D model of a basic car. This process taught me a lot about Fusion 360 and I am much more comfortable using it now. I learned things like setting parameters for more organized work, and how to duplicate parts instead of recreating them.
